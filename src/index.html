The Conscious and Subconscious Mind

It all starts with a proper understanding of the conscious and subconscious mind.

The conscious mind is the seat of intention. It sets the intention to draw—both the drawing as a whole and each of the marks that make it up.

The subconscious mind is the doer.

It responds to intention instantly and gives perfect feedback. It knows only the answer.

Your subconscious can guide you in marking off even the most complex or unusual proportions. It can guide you in placing the features of your subject correctly—even if it’s your first time drawing that subject. It can tell you exactly how much shading to add to a given area of your drawing. It can help you convey intricate, twisting forms, complex curvature, and subtle variations of depth.

And it can do all of this—easily, effortlessly, and naturally.

This course teaches you the language of the subconscious and gives you the tools, processes, and techniques needed to build a working rapport with it—your Inner Drawing Dynamo—so that you can channel this immense storehouse of power and experience the miracle of effortless drawing directly.

The Language of the Inner Drawing Dynamo

Your Inner Drawing Dynamo speaks in angles.

Whereas the brain often filters and distorts our perception of distance, angles can serve as a clear channel of communication between the conscious and subconscious mind.

There is therefore a world of difference between trying to fit a drawing together by measuring distances and fitting it together by perceiving angles.

The journey begins by learning how to set an intention to perceive an angle—and receive it in your hand.

You’ll then learn simple alignment gestures for engaging your subconscious mind to align along angles. Starting with simple subjects, and then progressing to more complex ones, you’ll apply the principle of triangulation to fit a drawing together—angle by angle—watching as it unfolds before your eyes.

Beyond Outlines: Building Form

But there’s more to drawing than making outlines.

While angles serve as the basis for outlining shapes, they can also be used in far more expressive ways—as the building blocks of form.

You’ll learn a simple gesture for crosshatching to shade a drawing, angling your hatch marks to suggest the intricacies of curvature and volume. Along the way, you’ll come to appreciate how crosshatching is, in essence, triangulation in three dimensions.

As your confidence grows, you’ll learn how to quickly establish the major and minor planes of your subject. You’ll then create a sense of unity and cohesion by applying the principle of parallel transport.

Seeing Light and Dark

And there’s still more.

Your Inner Drawing Dynamo is deeply attuned to the subtle variations of light and dark that make up your subject.

You’ll learn how to key your drawings to establish the areas of greatest light and dark. You’ll also learn a simple technique for querying your subconscious to determine the right amount of shading to apply in each area of your drawing.

Module 1

The Secret That Changes Everything

Many people struggle to draw, even after years of training and application. Many artists, moreover, can draw what they see only with great struggle and the aid of laborious measuring. Struggle, however, is unnecessary, for within each of us is an Inner Drawing Dynamo capable of effortless drawing—the subconscious. In this first module, you’ll receive the exact blueprint for transforming your relationship with your subconscious mind from an inner critic that relentlessly points out everything wrong with your drawing to a mighty, indispensable ally you can call upon for unfailing aid.

  • What makes drawing what you see so difficult for so many people, even many professionally trained artists
  • How the advice to draw what you see, not what you think you see, is misleading and frustrating
  • How your subconscious mind functions as the hidden gateway to effortless drawing
  • Why angles, rather than distances, serve as the bridge between the conscious and subconscious mind and are thus the natural tool for fitting a drawing together
  • How to set an intention to perceive an angle and receive immediate feedback from your subconscious mind—entering the feedback loop that is the foundation of creative flow
  • Simple practices to hone your drawing intuition

Module 2

Aligning Along Angles

In drawing, the most foundational skill is the ability to make marks along angles. This ability in turn makes it possible to replicate straight lines, and from straight lines spring complex contours and forms. You’ll learn just how easy it is to respond to an angle, capturing it in your hand and creating a rudder to serve as the basis for alignment. You’ll then learn simple gestures for initiating alignment and allowing the Drawing Dynamo Within to handle the details—effortlessly, elegantly, and with stunning accuracy.

  • How to effortlessly capture an angle in your hand
  • Simple alignment gestures for responding to and aligning along angles—eliminating the “offset” between your perception and your mark on the page
  • Techniques for replicating straight lines that don’t rely on perfect hand-eye coordination or a steady hand
  • How to break up a complex contour into a series of straight lines—the intuitive equivalent of calculus, performed naturally by your subconscious
  • How to perceive the endpoints of a straight line through intention and feedback, even when they are not explicitly marked

Module 3

First Steps in Triangulation

Every drawing, no matter how simple or complex, begins with two marks. To accurately convey the subject, all subsequent marks must be placed accurately in relation to these initial marks. Neither rigid nor abstract, triangulation gives us the practical and intuitive means of fitting a drawing together angle by angle and is the bridge between the conscious and subconscious mind.

  • Learn the principle of triangulation—from a natural and intuitive, rather than a mathematical perspective
  • Understand how triangulation inheres in every drawing method and is the unifying principle in drawing
  • Learn why the scale of a drawing—a choice made at the outset of the drawing process—is the only distance that matters, and why every subsequent mark can be placed using angles alone
  • Witness the miracle of triangulation unfold before your eyes, as you fit at first simple, then progressively more complex shapes together, angle by angle
  • Experience the flexibility and power of being able to start the same drawing in infinitely many ways
  • Set a micro-intention, receive visual feedback, and feel the flow state begin to emerge

Module 4

Short & Long Angles

The way you go about fitting angles together differs depending on whether you’re mapping out the overall shape and proportions of your subject or whether you’re focused on a particular area of a drawing. Short angles can be fitted together in quick succession using finger-based alignment gestures. Longer angles require greater finesse and understanding, yet they are the master key to unifying the different features of a subject.

  • Delve deeper into the concept of scale and how the choice of scale creates a distinction between short and long angles
  • Master advanced alignment gestures that naturally bring to bear the power of your entire arm, letting you draw easily and naturally from your shoulder
  • Learn when to apply different alignment techniques, depending on whether you’re aiming to establish placement and proportion or quickly convey small details
  • Tackle more advanced subjects, including portions of human and animal faces

Module 5

Advanced Triangulation

Equipped with the hands-on experience gained over the prior modules, you’re now ready to call upon your Drawing Dynamo Within to tackle far more ambitious and demanding subjects, including what many artists consider the most demanding subject of all: the human face. You’ll prove to yourself how even a beautiful and accurate portrait can arise effortlessly and naturally—almost of its own accord—from a blank page.

  • Demonstrate command and mastery of triangulation, as you efficiently read angles from photo reference and effortlessly draw even complex subjects—including pet and human portraits
  • Develop a sense of flow and fluidity, as you build more and more confidence in your intuitive drawing abilities
  • Surrender more and more of the drawing process to your Inner Drawing Dynamo
  • Discover anatomy through observation rather than relying on pre-memorized proportions or reference structures

Module 6

The Art of the Lay-In

Triangulation allows you to begin a drawing starting with any part of your subject. This module explores the different ways of beginning a drawing—otherwise known as the block-in or lay-in phase. For quick or casual drawings, you’ll learn simple techniques to establish key landmarks efficiently. For more elaborate drawings, you’ll practice the time-honored approach of working from big to small.

  • Learn different ways of starting off a drawing
  • Call upon your subconscious to create a lightning-fast freeform sketch to serve as the basis for a drawing
  • Discover the power of negative spaces for framing an image
  • Banish all remaining fear of the blank page
  • Plan out a drawing in advance, based on your requirements and desired results
  • Release the need for immediate accuracy and experience the joy of iterating on a drawing
  • Use the lay-in as a strategic entry point that confirms your composition—before you surrender fully to the subconscious flow

Module 7

Chiaroscuro

With this module, you move beyond the outline and into the dramatic world of light and shadow. Chiaroscuro—the art of exaggerating light and dark contrasts—is the key to drawings that leap off the page with striking three-dimensional impact. You’ll learn to establish a value scale calibrated to your medium and use muscle testing to query your subconscious for rapid, accurate decisions about which tones belong where.

  • Understand the essentials of light, dark, and value to shade a drawing effectively
  • Master a simple gesture for hatching straight lines to create patches of value
  • Build depth in your drawings by layering light patches of hatch marks upon one another
  • Learn the principle of Chiaroscuro and the importance of creating a strong separation of values
  • Learn how to create a value scale—whether 2-level, 3-level, or 5-level—calibrated to the capabilities of your specific medium
  • Use muscle testing to receive clear guidance from your subconscious about which value to choose for a specific area of a drawing

Module 8

Conveying Curvature and Volume

If triangulation is the language of 2D form, cross-hatching is its three-dimensional counterpart. Drawing on principles from differential geometry—translated into intuitive gesture—you’ll learn to convey volume, plane, and curvature through controlled rows of parallel lines. Your subconscious already performs these geometric calculations naturally; this module teaches you to trust that and let it flow.

  • Learn how cross-hatching creates the illusion of curvature and volume
  • Sculpt and create beautiful, flowing expressions of form that twist and merge intricately with one another
  • Understand how edges function as natural sources of curvature—providing the initial hatching angle your subconscious needs
  • Learn the secret of creating beautiful cross-contour lines by cutting into forms from different directions
  • Apply the principle of parallel transport to bring richness, depth, and unity to your cross-hatching
  • Bring together cross-hatching and Chiaroscuro to create simple, yet stunning illusions of depth
  • Immerse yourself in the joy of rendering, as you call upon your Inner Drawing Dynamo to create flow, depth, and richness

Module 9

Integration

In this culminating module, you’ll bring together triangulation, Chiaroscuro, and cross-hatching into a unified creative practice. Drawing becomes what it was always meant to be: a meditative cycle of conscious intention and subconscious response—a sacred feedback loop where you set an intention and watch it manifest through your hand.

  • Bring together all your newfound intuitive drawing techniques to create stunning pet and human portraits
  • Come to appreciate drawing as a process of manifestation—as a dance between conscious intention and subconscious feedback, made of innumerable small steps
  • Attain deeper meditative states while drawing, as you deepen your surrender to your Inner Drawing Dynamo
  • Establish a sacred drawing ritual that relaxes and revitalizes you
  • Step into the identity of the effortless draftsman—where the Inner Drawing Dynamo takes over and the act of drawing leaves you rejuvenated, not drained
